Proof of Consent

Proof of Consent for Demo SMS Communications

This page documents the verbal opt-in process used for a limited demo-only cybersecurity research study messaging environment.

Current scope: 2 controlled demo participants. This is not the production messaging program for the full study.

Operator and Study Context

The demo site and its associated SMS communications are operated by Jerson Francia with faculty oversight from Dr. Ben Schooley, in connection with a Brigham Young University affiliated cybersecurity research study demonstration.

The associated toll-free number is used only for controlled pilot messaging, infrastructure testing, and limited demonstration communications.

Opt-In Method

The opt-in method used for this demo is verbal consent. Participants are individually identified for the limited pilot and are not enrolled through this website.

Verbal Consent Script

Before any demo SMS is sent, the researcher reads the following script to the participant:

Hello, this is Jerson Francia working on a BYU-affiliated cybersecurity research study demo. We are asking for your permission to send a small number of SMS messages to this phone number for a controlled demonstration of our study messaging system. These messages are for testing only, message frequency is low, and message and data rates may apply. You can reply STOP at any time to opt out or HELP for assistance. Do you agree to receive these demo SMS messages at this number?

Example Consent Exchange

Researcher: [Reads the verbal consent script above.]

Participant: Yes.

Demo messages are sent only after the participant gives an explicit affirmative verbal response.

What Participants Are Told Before Messaging Begins

  • They may receive limited SMS messages related to a cybersecurity research study demo.
  • The messages are part of a controlled pilot used to validate the study messaging infrastructure.
  • Message frequency is low and limited to the demo environment.
  • Message and data rates may apply.
  • They may reply STOP to opt out of future demo messages.
  • They may reply HELP for assistance.

When Consent Is Collected

Verbal consent is obtained before any demo message is sent. Only after consent is provided are participant phone numbers used for the limited pilot messaging workflow.

The current demo is restricted to two controlled participants for infrastructure validation and demonstration purposes.

How Verbal Consent Is Recorded

After the participant gives an explicit verbal yes, the operator records the consent in a private study log before any SMS is sent. The log includes:

  • participant name or demo identifier
  • phone number provided for the demo
  • date and time consent was obtained
  • name of the operator who collected consent
  • whether the participant explicitly agreed to receive the demo SMS messages

Only phone numbers with a recorded affirmative consent entry are used for the limited demo.

Confirmation Message After Opt-In

If the operator chooses to send a confirmation message after verbal opt-in, the message uses this format:

You are opted in to receive limited demo messages for the BYU Cybersecurity Study Demo. Message frequency is low. Message and data rates may apply. Reply STOP to opt out or HELP for help.
